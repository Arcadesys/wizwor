# Notable NES/Famicom romhacks (curated, not exhaustive)

This is intentionally short. "Every romhack ever made" isn't a real, bounded list —
romhacking.net alone catalogs several thousand NES/Famicom hacks, with new ones
made regularly by the community. Anyone (me included) claiming to enumerate "all"
of them from memory would be fabricating entries to look complete. Instead, this
is a small set of hacks/translations I have reasonable confidence actually exist
and are well documented — treat even these as "go verify," not gospel.

For the real, current, comprehensive catalog: **romhacking.net's NES section** is
the actual source of truth, searchable by category (translation, improvement,
total conversion, etc.) with descriptions and download info.

## Fan translations (Japan-only Famicom titles brought to English)
- Mother (Famicom) — fan translation of the Japan-only prequel to EarthBound
- Fire Emblem: Shadow Dragon and the Blade of Sword (Famicom) — fan translation of the original Fire Emblem
- Final Fantasy II & III (Famicom) — fan translations (distinct from the renumbered SNES/US releases)

## Notable improvement/overhaul hacks
- Mega Man 4: Minus Infinity — a large, well-regarded overhaul/expansion hack of Mega Man 4

## Notable fan games / homebrew built on NES hardware or hacked engines
- Metroid: Rogue Dawn — fan-made prequel built on a heavily modified Metroid engine

## What I'd suggest instead of trying to grow this list from memory
If you want real breadth here, the practical path is pulling from romhacking.net
directly (browsing/searching their NES category) rather than me trying to recall
more names — anything past this short list starts trading confidence for volume.
